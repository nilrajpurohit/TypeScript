1. What is Utility:
    In TypeScript, “utility types” are built-in generic types that help you transform and reuse existing types instead of rewriting them.

2. Types of Utility:
    - Partial: Partial<T>
    - Required: Required<T>
    - Readonly: Readonly<T>
    - Pick: Pick<T, K>
    - Omit: Omit<T, K>
    - Record: Record<K, T>
    - Exclude: Exclude<T, K>
    - Extract: Extract<T, U>
    - NonNullable: NonNullable<T>
    - ReturnType: ReturnType<T>
    - Parameters: Parameters<T>
    - Awaited: Awaited<T>
